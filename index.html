The Quest

by: Erik Earl

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I made this game to challenge the player and my own abilities in game development. I chose this style because I like story and skill type games.

2. What I Learned

I learned from my testing that my game started off being way too difficult, so I ended up giving the player more health and adding items like the shield in order to make it possible to beat it.

3. Architecture of My Game

I made the game's map a grid that allows you to walk into certain squares after completing challenges. The classes work mostly as molds for different enemies and objects in the game for the player to fight.

4. Technical Implementation

I liked the rooms and 2D style movement implemented in my game. The map is laid out like a grid and has restricted areas that are blocked off until they are unlocked by the player in order to add to the story and guide the player through the game. While I had difficulties reaching the final state of the map, ultimately, after many additions and revisions I was satisfied with the world.

5. Most Challenging Part

The most challenging part of this project was implementing movement for different objects like enemies with different paths and having multiple projectiles on the screen at once. I worked around my problems by distinguishing enemies based on their movement type and using the location of the player relative to the enemy to dictate certain paths for objects to take.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

If I were to continue to work on my game I would add more story and more items for the player to use. I would also add new enemies and bosses as the game is limited in its variety.
